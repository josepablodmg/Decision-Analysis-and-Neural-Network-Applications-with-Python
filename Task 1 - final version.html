Problem:¶

Cash-Without-Borders A multi-national fintech company which offers payment solutions for private consumers as well as small- to medium-enterprises considers lowering their cost of technical support. The payment solution company has the strategy of offering payments transfers quick and in a hassle-free way. For technical support, the company consider the strategies: keep the support in-house as before, outsource to a call-centre in a third country or apply a specially trained chat- bot. The advantage of having the support in-house is that the company will lose high-value clients if the service becomes worse.

a) Do a SMART-analysis of the problem as described in Chapter 3, Decisions involving multiple objectives in the book Decision Analysis for Management Judgment on eStudy. Be sure to include a value-tree for the problem.¶

SMART Analysis¶

Specific: The objective is to lower the cost of technical support while maintaining a high quality of service to avoid losing high-value clients.

Measurable: The success can be measured by comparing the total costs of technical support and tracking customer satisfaction scores.

Achievable: The goal is achievable by either keeping the support in-house, outsourcing to a third country, or using a chatbot.

Relevant: Reducing costs is crucial for the company to remain competitive and profitable.

Time-bound: The decision should be implemented within a specific time frame, for instance, within the next fiscal year.

Alternatives:¶

Keep support in-house Outsource to a call center in a third country Implement a specially trained chatbot

Value tree

The value tree for the problem can be divided into the main objective and sub-objectives that contribute to achieving the main goal.

Main Objective: Reduce technical support costs while maintaining service quality.:**

Minimize Costs: Staff salaries Operational expenses Training and development

Maximize Customer Satisfaction:

Response time Issue resolution effectiveness Personalization of service

Manage Risk:

Risk of losing high-value clients Risk of technical issues Risk of implementation delays

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

costs = {
    "in_house": 1000000,
    "outsourced": 600000,
    "chatbot": 300000
}

risks = {
    "in_house": (0.05, 500000),
    "outsourced": (0.15, 500000),
    "chatbot": (0.10, 500000)
}

quality_costs = {
    "in_house": 0,
    "outsourced": 100000,
    "chatbot": 150000
}

# Here we do the sensitivity analysis for risk probabilities

risk_probabilities = np.linspace(0, 0.2, 11)
emv_in_house_probs = []
emv_outsourced_probs = []
emv_chatbot_probs = []

for prob in risk_probabilities:
    emv_in_house_probs.append(costs['in_house'] + quality_costs['in_house'] + (prob * risks['in_house'][1]))
    emv_outsourced_probs.append(costs['outsourced'] + quality_costs['outsourced'] + (prob * risks['outsourced'][1]))
    emv_chatbot_probs.append(costs['chatbot'] + quality_costs['chatbot'] + (prob * risks['chatbot'][1]))

# Here we are plotting the sensitivity analysis for risk probabilities

plt.figure(figsize=(14, 6))

plt.plot(risk_probabilities, emv_in_house_probs, label='In-house', marker='o')
plt.plot(risk_probabilities, emv_outsourced_probs, label='Outsourced', marker='o')
plt.plot(risk_probabilities, emv_chatbot_probs, label='Chatbot', marker='o')

plt.xlabel('Risk Probability')
plt.ylabel('EMV')
plt.title('Sensitivity to Risk Probability')
plt.legend()
plt.grid(True)
plt.show()

The graphs show that as the probability of losing high-value clients increases, the EMV for each strategy also increases. However, the chatbot strategy consistently maintains a lower EMV compared to the in-house and outsourced strategies across the entire range of probabilities (0 to 0.2).

This reaffirms the decision to choose the chatbot strategy for reducing technical support costs while maintaining service quality. By consistently offering the lowest EMV, the chatbot strategy stands out as the optimal choice.
